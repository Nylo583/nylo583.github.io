Fannergy

August 2022
<< back to blog directory

 My first completed Hackathon was for the World World Youth Coders Hackathon. I remember one of my friends (let's call him S) proposed, as a joke, to create an AI fan for the theme of “Beating the Heat”. We called up our third group member in Canada (Mr. C for no other reason) and production was underway.

 We had an entire week (wrong, we wasted 2 days on the idea) to create an integrated smart fan, complete with a website interface. I would handle the AI model and the website, C would work on the web server, and S would tackle sensing and hardware.

 It took me around a day to set up the Tensorflow model in Python and train it on systematically generated data. The end goal of the Fannergy system was to spin the fan at a pace which would attempt to cancel out changes in room temperature and humidity and keep the room at a pleasant 70 degrees Fahrenheit.

 If I had more time, I would've pulled data from an API to make a better dataset, but I went with the “random-number-and-calculate” dataset generation method. In the end, it worked out with a 0.05% margin of error after 500 or so epochs of training.

 It was now day 4 and time to get started on the website. Our team ended up deciding to create a home page, ID login, and a control board page which would either set a manual fan speed or give way to the AI to make decisions for the user. It would also include JS graphs of historical temperature and humidity readings. This was my first expedition into making websites, so it took me the great deal of the rest of the last 4 days to come up with a somewhat-pleasing-to-the-eye interface.
