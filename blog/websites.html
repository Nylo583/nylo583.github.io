 This page combines every website I've made so far into one page, from 2022 to today.

 -- Fannergy
 See the associated blog post.

 -- CSHS
 This website was originally created for my high school's Computer Science Honor Society, which I was lucky to be a co-vice president of in its initial years. The website itself was just a bunch of static pages that would be used to display news, check volunteering hours, and contact leadership. This was my first real foray into actual web + graphic design, and while the site never launched, I do consider it to have been a good instructional experience.

 -- Northville Mobile Detailing
 Done as freelance work during my sophomore and junior years of high school, I worked with a small-scale family business to create a website for their detailing services. The project initially began with a modern, techy look that tried to encapsulate the feel of their cutting-edge cleaning technology.

 This version was unfortunately scrapped as the clients put the job on hold for around a year, and I ended up redesigning the entire site into a cleaner, more professional look. On the final version of the site, you can see a completely custom responsive design including a collapsing navbar and a custom image carousel. You can view the full website here.

 -- TeamButter (Internship @ Cimulus, Inc.)
 This was part of my high school's internship program, which I participated in during my final semester. I was able to secure the internship with a small-scale software solutions company in Ann Arbor. Part of my internship schedule included a rotation of duties until I found a good place within the company, and I ended up landing in ASP.NET MVC Core development. My main task over these fifteen or so weeks was to migrate a live-service website from Blazor to MVC Core as the maintenance of the current site was far too much. Over the course of the internship, I successfully migrated the DIY subdomain -- that is, the workflow you enter when signing into the page -- and linked the frontend forms to the underlying databases. While I won't provide screenshots as this is proprietary code, you can visit the non-migrated site here.

 -- Personal Website
 I have designed many iterations of a personal portfolio website, with the oldest ranging back to around 2021. The only one that has ever made it to the actual internet, besides this one, was the personal website I designed in my junior year. The website features a one-page layout with scroll snapping, an interactible gallery, and most impressively, a projects page that loaded separate posts using only CSS (I was once a JavaScript hater). As you might be able to tell from the video below, however, the site layout did not lend itself nicely to be made responsive, triggering the development of my current site.

 This site, taking note of the failures of the last, uses Bootstrap 5 to cleanly display my content responsively. The gallery is a Masonry layout combined with responsive Bootstrap scaling. In the future, I am considering writing a custom script to load projects and blogs automatically, instead of hardcodoing them.
